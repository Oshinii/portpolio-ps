Learning Outcome

Surviving Skills

The “Blizzard at Basecamp” lecture provided an interactive survival exercise designed to strengthen leadership and teamwork skills in high-pressure environments. In the scenario, a team stranded in the Himalayas during a severe blizzard had to select only eight critical items from a survival list to endure at least three days until help arrived. The activity required participants to balance survival essentials such as warmth, food, water, shelter, and communication with team morale and safety.


Beyond survival, the exercise reinforced vital professional skills like strategic decision-making, consensus building, and risk management. Each team member was expected to take on roles such as leader, recorder, timekeeper, or presenter, promoting accountability and cooperation. By justifying each choice based on necessity and skill development, students learned to evaluate limited resources, manage conflicts, and adapt strategies under uncertainty. The lecture highlighted that survival success depends not only on tools and resources but also on effective leadership, clear communication, and the ability to make rational, collective decisions under pressure skills directly transferable to corporate and organizational settings.
