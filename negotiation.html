Learning Outcome

Negotiation Skills

This lecture provides a deep exploration into negotiation as a critical competency for business and daily interactions. It begins with an introduction to the concepts, definitions, and significance of negotiation, highlighting how it serves as a structured process to resolve conflicts, create value, and build sustainable agreements. Core principles such as preparation, communication, mutual respect, and ethical conduct are emphasized, along with the analytical frameworks of Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement and Zone of Possible Agreement, which guide decision-making and define the boundaries of successful outcomes.


The session further explores strategies such as distributive and integrative negotiation, and techniques including active listening, persuasion, building trust, and framing proposals effectively. By incorporating practical exercises like role plays and experience sharing, the workshop strengthens participants’ problem-solving ability, adaptability, and emotional intelligence, ensuring they can approach negotiations with confidence, creativity, and resilience in diverse real-world scenarios.
